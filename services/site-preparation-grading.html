Why The Grade Matters More Than Most People Think

A bad grade shows up the first time it rains, the first time the slab cracks, the first time the foundation shifts. Most building problems trace back to grading that was rushed or done wrong from the start. Our work prevents the issues that catch builders and homeowners off guard years later.

Warning Signs Site Prep Wasn't Done Right

Bad site prep doesn't show up the day the build is done. It shows up months or years later, when it costs ten times as much to fix.

  • Water pooling near the foundation that wasn't there during construction
  • Cracked slabs or sagging concrete from improper compaction underneath
  • Standing water in low spots that the original grade should have addressed
  • Sloping that runs toward the building instead of away from it

What To Know Before We Start Your Site Prep

Weather And Timing

Heavy rain or frozen ground may push grading work for quality reasons. We schedule around the weather so the prep holds.

Coordination With Other Trades

Site prep timing often ties to concrete, framing, and utility schedules. We work with your builder or GC to line the work up right.

Realistic Timelines

Small pads and simple grading projects run 1-3 days. Full new-build site prep with rough and final grading may run 1-2 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often from property owners planning a project.

How long does site prep take?

A simple pad or small grading job usually runs one to three days. Full site prep for a new build with rough and final grade can run one to two weeks depending on the size of the footprint and the conditions.

Do you handle both rough grading and final grade?

Yes. We can take a site from rough grade at the start of a build all the way to final grade ahead of concrete, asphalt, or landscaping. A lot of outfits skip the finish work; we don't.

Can you prep a pad for a pole barn or outbuilding?

That's a big part of what we do. We level and prep the pad, plan the drainage into the grade, and set it up so the build sits right and water moves away from it.

Will you coordinate with my builder or concrete crew?

Yes. Site prep timing usually ties into framing, concrete, and utility schedules. We'll work with your builder or GC to line the grade up so it's ready when the next crew shows up.

Why does the grade matter so much?

A bad grade shows up the first time it rains, the first time a slab cracks, the first time a foundation shifts. Getting the grade and drainage right at the start prevents the expensive problems that surface years later.
