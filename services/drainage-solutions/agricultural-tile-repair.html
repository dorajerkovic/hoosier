Where Field Tile Usually Fails

Old tile systems break down for predictable reasons. Knowing where to look saves time and money in the repair.

  • Crushed or collapsed tile from heavy equipment, settling, or root intrusion
  • Sediment-blocked tile that's been slowly filling in over years
  • Disconnected joints where original installation has worked loose
  • Failed outlets where tile meets a ditch, creek, or surface drainage point

How We Repair Field Tile

Field tile repair starts with locating the actual failure point. The wet spot in the field is usually downhill from where the tile actually broke.

1

Walk the field and identify wet spots, slow-draining areas, or known problem zones

2

Locate the tile run and excavate to expose the failure point

3

Repair or replace the section, then backfill and restore the surface

Warning Signs Of Failing Tile

Tile problems start small but show up clearly once the spring rains hit.

  • Wet spots in the field that didn't used to be wet
  • Slow drainage in areas that previously drained quickly
  • Surface erosion in patterns that suggest underground water movement
  • Settled or sunken ground along known tile lines
