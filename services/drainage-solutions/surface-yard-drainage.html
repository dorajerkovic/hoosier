Where Surface Drainage Problems Show Up

Most surface drainage issues come from grade problems that were either ignored when the property was developed or got worse over time. These are the situations we work on most.

  • Yards that stay saturated for days after rain, even in well-draining soil
  • Standing water in low spots that never drains or evaporates fast enough
  • Runoff cutting paths through landscaping, gravel, or sloped areas
  • Water flowing toward the foundation, garage, or outbuilding

Warning Signs Your Property Needs Surface Drainage Work

Surface drainage problems get worse season by season. A wet spot this year becomes a foundation problem the next.

  • Mud and erosion paths that show up every spring
  • Dead landscaping or grass in areas that stay wet too long
  • Water stains or moisture on basement or foundation walls
  • Mosquito problems in summer from water that won't dry out

How We Fix Surface Drainage Problems

Surface drainage work starts with figuring out where the water is actually coming from and where it should be going. The fix follows from there.

1

Walk the property during or after rain to track water movement

2

Identify the source: grade issue, runoff path, or downspout location

3

Regrade and shape the surface to direct water to a proper drainage point
